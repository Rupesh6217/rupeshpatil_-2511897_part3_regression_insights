# Final Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Regression analysis was conducted to identify the key factors associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores. Multiple regression was selected as the final model because it provided the highest explanatory power (R² = 0.811) and incorporated several business-relevant variables simultaneously.

## Key Factors Associated with Monthly Sales

The analysis indicates that the following variables have the strongest positive association with monthly sales:

1. Footfall
2. Marketing Spend
3. Inventory Availability Percentage
4. Regional Factors (South and West regions)

Among these variables, footfall emerged as the strongest predictor of sales performance.

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

### Increase Customer Footfall

Stores with higher customer traffic consistently generate higher sales. Leadership should invest in customer acquisition campaigns, local promotions, loyalty programs, and store visibility initiatives.

### Optimize Marketing Investment

Marketing spend shows a positive relationship with sales. Marketing budgets should be allocated strategically toward regions and stores demonstrating stronger returns.

### Maintain High Inventory Availability

Inventory availability contributes positively to sales performance. Reducing stockouts and improving supply chain efficiency can support revenue growth.

### Regional Strategy

Stores located in South and West regions appear to outperform the East region after controlling for other variables. Regional business strategies should be reviewed to identify best practices that can be replicated elsewhere.

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Some variables may show weak statistical significance or may be influenced by external factors not captured in the dataset. Leadership should avoid making major strategic decisions based solely on regional differences or isolated coefficient values without additional business validation.

## Recommended Business Actions

* Increase customer traffic through targeted campaigns.
* Improve inventory management processes.
* Use data-driven allocation of marketing budgets.
* Conduct further investigation into high-performing regions.
* Monitor store-level operational practices that may not be represented in the model.

## Risks and Limitations

The model explains approximately 81% of the variation in monthly sales; however, not all influencing factors are included. External variables such as local competition, economic conditions, weather, customer demographics, and promotional events may also affect performance.

## Association Versus Causation

Regression analysis identifies statistical associations between variables. It does not automatically prove that one variable directly causes another. Business decisions should therefore combine regression insights with operational expertise, experimentation, and managerial judgment.

## Final Conclusion

The multiple regression model is recommended as the primary analytical model because it explains the highest proportion of monthly sales variation and provides the most useful guidance for business decision-making. Leadership should prioritize initiatives that increase footfall, optimize marketing expenditure, and maintain strong inventory availability.
